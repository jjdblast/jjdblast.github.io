GreenBox

A “Senseable” Way To Plant

Growing plants all by yourself can be one of the most rewarding things you’ll ever do. For harvesting, we need plenty of time and experience, so how about growing plants under the guidance of AI?

The key points of designing an AI planting platform are Sensing and Inferencing. Sensing means to monitor and digitalize the status of plants; Inferencing means to make a series of decisions, including irrigation fertilization, and illumination.

GreenBox is designed to solve the Sensing problem, it is a tabletop-sized and easy-to-build planting platform with smart robotic systems to control the environment, climate, and monitor the status of the plant growth; For solving the Inferencing problem, I also designed GreenBox-KG, which is a knowledge graph built by data collected from GreenBox to make better decisions in the planting process.



GreenBox Demo Video


GreenBox

The main shell of GreenBox is a tiny greenhouse equipped with multiple environment sensors, includes CO2, air temperature, humidity, root-zone temperature, and electrical conductivity, sensors’s data will be collected and analyzed on Raspberry Pi.



GreenBox Shell

Here’s all of sensors used in GreenBox:

  • Environment sensors
    • air tempreture & humdity: BME280 Environmental Sensor
    • carbon dioxide: Infrared CO2 Sensor
  • Root-zone sensors
    • soil tempreture & humdity & EC: RS485 Soil Sensor
    • soil PH: RS485 Soil PH sensor
  • Light sensors
    • spectrum sensors: SparkFun Triad Spectroscopy Sensor
  • Other sensors or controllers
    • arduino uno:
    • raspberry pi 3b+:
    • camera: RPi IR-CUT Camera
    • water pumps, led strips are DIY


SparkFun Triad Spectroscopy Sensor

In order to analyze fine-grained illumination condition, we use spectrum sensors to collect 18 channels spectral response of environment light inside the box.



GreenBox Light Unit

For light control, I use 2 WS2811B 16x16 LED matrix strips to build light unit, which could also work as a heater. The LED matrix could be controlled element-wise by arduino.



Sensors & “Air Pump”



Soil Sensor & Water Pump

For irigation, a small water pump will drill water from extra water bins once the soil humdity drop below the red line. The Soild Sensor is placed under the soil, other sensors are fixed around the box. Also, I use a tiny raspberry fans as a low-cost air pump.




GreenBox Data Visualization

I use raspberry pi 3b+ to collect sensors’s data and comunicate with arduino, the actual control task is done by arduino. In order to monitor and analysis the whole system, I wrote a service on RPI to store all sensors’s data in SQLite DB in realtime, then deploy Apache Superset to provide data management and visulization, all softwares and servcies are running on RPI, so it’s pretty energy efficient. It’s cost is also affordable for student, now my hardware costs for GreenBox version 1.0 is about 200$.

GreenBox-KG

Since I haven’t collect enough growing data when I started this project, so a solution for cold-starting) is to crawl open-source agriculture common knowledge data, such as wikipedia. In this project, I found hudong baike is very useful, which is a chinese version wikipedia contains many domain knowledge, I crawled agriculture related subjects from hudong baike, table below shows some samples.



Hudong Baike Sample Records

With those records, we can find the suitable climate for each crops/plants growing, which means we can also get a coarse range of suitable temperature for crops/plants growth. For those crops/plants only have growing suitable regions, we need match the climate with the regions.



Global Climate Types

So I crawled climate type of regions from Köppen climate classification), such as Beijing is subtropical monsoon climate, which means it’s has a qualifying average temperature in the warmest month of greater than 22 °C.



Building Knowledge Graph in Neo4J DB

After simple data preprocessing, all data was saved into Neo4J DB. A simple knowledge graph can be built using Neo4J , I also implemented a KBQA system. For now we’ve already had an algriculture knowledge graph, we could use this to get some coarse planting advises ( say, “What crops are suitable for planting in Beijing?” or “ What is the optimum temperature for basil growth?”).

Now we can start to grow a plant at home! After register on RPI based server, I can monitor my plant in realtime, some simple irrigation and ilumination strategy is already implemented on GreenBox, we just need to make sure everything works well in this tiny box.

Once we get a good harvest, we can use this growing data as a success profile for this type of plant, the more profile we collect, the more intelligent strategy can we design. Based on large amout of data, we can get more strong fine-grained KBQA sytem, which may solve more complicated questions like “If I want to get a sweeter basil, what temperature range should I set ?” or “I want to save 20% of water used by irrigation, what ilumination strategy should I use ?”.
